Biography

Jordan grew up between cultures, a childhood split between continents that would later define the kinds of stories he sought out as a filmmaker. He studied at the American Film Institute, where he developed a visceral, character-forward visual language — one rooted in naturalistic light and the dignity of the human face.

His feature debut, Typical, premiered to widespread critical acclaim, described by the Village Voice as a masterwork of quiet tension. His documentary work — particularly Ancestors of Tomorrow — has screened at festivals worldwide and brought international attention to indigenous land rights movements.

As a DP, Jordan's commercial and branded work is defined by the same cinematic rigor he brings to personal projects. His collaborations with Nike, IKEA, Calvin Klein, and a roster of groundbreaking artists have earned him a reputation as one of the most sought-after visual storytellers working today.
